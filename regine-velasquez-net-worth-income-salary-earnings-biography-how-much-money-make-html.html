Regine Velasquez (born April 22, 1970) is a Filipina music singer and actress who is well-known for having a broad vocal range. She is well known for her song “It’s a Love Story.” She was named the Philippines’ Best-Selling Artist of All Time by the Philippine Association of the Record Industry (PARI), with more than 7 million records sold in the Philippines and 1.5 million albums sold throughout Asia. During the 1989 Asia Pacific Singing Contest in Hong Kong, Velasquez was awarded the Grand Prize. Listen Without Prejudice, her first album to be published in Asia, was produced by Polygram Records in 199After selling more than 700,000 copies in Asia, it is widely regarded as Velasquez’s most successful album to this day. Sales of the album have topped 100,000 sales in the Philippines and 300,000 units in China, with just 20,000 units sold in Thailand. In 2000, Velasquez’s music video for “In Love With You,” a duet with Jacky Cheung, debuted at number one on MTV Asia’s list of the Top 20 Asian Videos, which he shared with Cheung. On the eve of the new century, as part of the BBC’s Millennium Special 2000 Today, Velasquez sang the Philippine millennium theme, “Written in the Sand,” with the help of around 2,000 Filipino teenagers in the United Kingdom. This was aired by the BBC to 67 nations, reaching approximately a billion people in all of the world. As part of Carnegie Hall’s centennial-year performance series, Velasquez makes history by being the first Asian musician to perform a solo concert at the venerable New York institution’s Carnegie Hall. She becomes the first Filipino to win the Favorite Artist award at the MTV Asia Awards, which were held in Singapore in 200The following year, at the 2003 MTV Asia Awards, she was presented with the same prize. She is the first Filipino artist to have a special interview with Lorraine Hahn on CNN Talk Asia, which took place in 200Velasquez is the first Filipino musician to perform a solo performance at the Mall of Asia Arena, which took place in 201Other international artists with whom she has worked include Paul Anka, David Hasselhoff, 98 Degrees, Alicia Keys, Brian McKnight, Mandy Moore, Rondy Moore, Stephen Bishop, Jim Brickman, Peabo Bryson, Jeffrey Osborne, Dave Koz, Coco Lee, Michel Legrand, Eduardo Capetillo, Fernando Carrillo, David Archuleta, Russell Watson and Singaporean vocal band Skritch. She has also performed and collaborated with other international artists In addition to the MTV Asia Awards (in Singapore), the UNICEF Benefit Concert (in China), the Perfect 10 Music Awards (in Singapore), the Asia Live Dream Concert (in Japan), the SEA Games, Asian Music Scene, Miss Universe Preliminaries (in the Philippines), the Lunar New Year Parade and Festival (in the United States), the Asian TV Awards (in Singapore), and the Mosaic International Music Festival, she has also performed at numerous international events (in Singapore). Additionally, she has worked on several television and film projects, winning a Best Actress award for her portrayal of a mentally challenged woman in the episode “Lobo” of the Maalaala Mo Kaya television series, as well as another Best Actress award for her role in the film Of All the Things. Her film Pangako Ikaw Lang earned her the title of “Box-Office Queen” for the first time.
